# Food & Dining - Agra Local Knowledge

## Street Food
- **Bedai & Jalebi** – Deviram Sweets (near Raja Mandi)
  - Best time: 7-10 AM
  - Famous among locals for fresh morning breakfast
- **Petha** – Panchhi Petha (original and most famous)
  - Multiple varieties: plain, chocolate, coconut
  - Must-try for visitors
- **Chaat** – Sadar Bazaar, Shah Market
  - Golgappa, bhel puri, aloo tikki
  - Evening time best (5-9 PM)

## Restaurants
- **Pinch of Spice** (North Indian)
  - Location: Near Taj Ganj
  - Good for families, AC dining
- **Joney's Place** (Budget-friendly)
  - Location: Near Taj Ganj
  - Popular with backpackers and budget travelers
- **Dasaprakash** (South Indian)
  - Famous for dosa and South Indian breakfast
  - Clean and hygienic

## Local Specialties
- **Agra Petha** - Sweet made from ash gourd
- **Bedai** - Deep fried bread with spicy potato curry
- **Dalmoth** - Spicy lentil snack mix
- **Gajak** - Sesame and jaggery sweet (winter special)

## Local Food Habits
- Locals prefer street food in morning (7-10 AM) and evening (5-9 PM)
- Breakfast culture is strong - bedai, jalebi, kachori
- Evening snacks at Sadar Bazaar are very popular
- Petha is bought as gift for relatives when leaving Agra

## Areas for Food
- **Sadar Bazaar** - Street food hub, evening snacks
- **Shah Market** - Local chaat and snacks
- **Raja Mandi** - Morning breakfast spots
- **Taj Ganj** - Tourist-friendly restaurants
